Culture Scope

The scope of eCultures Kenya extends across Kenya’s 42 ethnic groups, embracing their traditions, arts, music, rituals, languages, and culinary practices. Our focus is not just on documentation, but on contextual storytelling — capturing how culture evolves and remains relevant in today’s digital era.

From the highlands of Kikuyu and Kalenjin lands to the coastal Swahili culture and pastoralist heritage of the Maasai and Samburu, we explore Kenya’s shared human experiences. Our platform serves schools, researchers, and communities who wish to understand Kenya through the lens of diversity and unity.
